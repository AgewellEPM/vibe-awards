The Vibe Awards Entry Agreement

Simple. Fair. Transparent.

Version 1.0 - Plain English

1 The Deal

When you join a Vibe Awards challenge, you're entering a partnership:

🤝 The 95/5 Split
You and your team keep 95% of everything you build.
The Vibe Awards keeps 5% as our partnership share.

This applies to any IP, code, designs, or company created during the challenge period.

🏗️

🚀

📺

🏆
Awards & Glory
Emmy-level recognition

3 Your Rights

  • You own 95% of everything you create
  • You can continue building after the challenge ends
  • You can raise funding, sell, or pivot freely
  • You keep all prize money won
  • You can use anything you build in your portfolio
  • You decide how to split equity among your team

4 Our Rights

  • We keep 5% equity/IP in projects created during challenges
  • We can showcase your project publicly
  • We can connect you with investors and sponsors
  • We can include you in marketing materials
  • We can run the awards show and battles

5 The Simple Rules

  • One person, one role, one team per challenge
  • All work must be original (no pre-existing code)
  • Teams must submit before the timer expires
  • Be cool to your teammates
  • No illegal stuff

Common Questions

Why 5%? ▼
It's the smallest meaningful stake that aligns our incentives. Y Combinator takes 7%, most accelerators take 10-20%. We provide the platform, matching, tools, and spotlight - 5% is our partnership fee for making it all happen.
What if my team wants to continue after the challenge? ▼
Awesome! That's the goal. You own 95% and can do whatever you want - raise money, hire people, pivot, sell. We'll even help connect you with VCs. Our 5% just comes along for the ride.
What if we don't want to form a company? ▼
No problem! The 5% only matters if your project becomes something real. If it's just a fun weekend project that ends there, everyone had a good time and learned something. No strings attached beyond the challenge.
Can I use my own existing code or libraries? ▼
Open source libraries and frameworks are fine (React, Next.js, etc). But the core project logic must be original work created during the challenge. No copy-pasting your previous startup.
Who owns what within the team? ▼
That's up to you! The team owns 95% collectively and can split it however you agree. We recommend equal splits to keep it simple, but it's your call.
